Redesign Bus Stops in Ann Arbor
For a more inclusive, informative, and comfortable public transportation experience.

Team Members: Alex Wang, Jean Avaala, Brian Richards

Problem Statement

How might we make bus stops more accessible, comfortable and/or informative for passengers to improve their riding experience?

The Riders team focused on developing a solution to the problem of public transportation in the city of Ann Arbor, addressing sustainable development goals. We narrowed the problem space and focused more specifically on bus stops (visibility at bus stops, seating at bus stops and information at bus stops). Through stakeholder engagement, we landed on our final solution of designing a policy brief for The Ann Arbor Area Transportation Authority (“The Ride”) detailing possible solutions for affordable, comfortable seating at bus stops, improving visibility at bus stops and more informative bus stop signs. The process we embarked on to reach this solution is outlined in the sections that follow.

Sustainable Goals

Our project aligns with SDG 9 (Infrastructure) and SDG 11 (Sustainable Cities), focusing on equitable access to safe and affordable transit, especially for marginalized communities. By improving bus stop design, we support mobility, inclusion, and community well-being.

Research Process

Photo Ethnography

We conducted field observations of bus stops and documented problems in context.

Stakeholder Engagement

We spoke with TheRide officials, local riders, and city stakeholders to uncover real needs.

AEIOU + Survey

We used structured observation and public surveys to gather and validate data.

Synthesis & HMW

Framed insights as "How Might We..." questions to guide solution space.

Idea Generation

Used individual brainstorming and Design Heuristics to generate 100+ ideas.

Selection & Prototyping

We selected feasible ideas across visibility, comfort, and information to prototype.

Final Design & Deliverables

Our final outcome is a modular, branded bus stop sign with real-time tracking and updated maps. We also proposed seat and visibility enhancements and compiled all recommendations in a Policy Brief sent to TheRide. Our design was evaluated using real rider feedback and aligned with other global transit signage best practices.
